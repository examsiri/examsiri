CBSE Exams

The Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) is a national level board of education in India for public and private schools, controlled and managed by the Government of India. Established in 1929 by a resolution of the government, the Board was an experiment towards inter-state integration and cooperation in the sphere of secondary education. There are more than 27,000 schools in India and 240 schools in 28 foreign countries affiliated to the CBSE. All schools affiliated to CBSE follow the NCERT curriculum especially from class 9 to 12.

Gujarat State Board

Gujarat State Board is a state-level education board responsible for conducting secondary (class 10) and higher secondary (class 12) examinations in the state of Gujarat, India. The Gujarat Secondary and Higher Secondary Education Board (GSEB) is the primary body responsible for conducting these exams.
The board was established in the year 1960 and is headquartered in Gandhinagar, the capital city of Gujarat. The GSEB is responsible for prescribing the syllabus and textbooks for the schools affiliated with it, conducting exams, publishing results, and granting recognition to schools in the state.

Haryana State Board

The Directorate of Government Examinations or the Andhra Pradesh Board of Secondary Education is the main education Board in the state of Andhra Pradesh. The Andhra Pradesh Board of Secondary Education (BSEAP), which is also known as the AP SSC Board conducts the Class 10 board exams in the state. And the Board of Intermediate Education (BIE) conducts the Andhra Intermediate or Class 12 exams. In addition to this, the AP Board is also responsible for prescribing the syllabus, curriculum and textbooks for Class 1 to Class 12 students in the state. Another important function of the AP Board is to evaluate the Class 10 and Class 12 Board exam and announce the results.
